Formula 1 Data Science Project

2024 Season Analysis using FastF1 API

Introduction

Formula 1 racing represents the pinnacle of motorsport engineering and athletic performance, where milliseconds determine victory and defeat. The sport combines cutting-edge automotive technology, aerodynamic innovation, strategic decision-making, and human skill at speeds exceeding 350 kilometers per hour. Beyond the spectacle of racing, Formula 1 generates vast amounts of data from telemetry systems, timing sensors, and performance metrics that capture every aspect of car and driver performance. This data provides unprecedented opportunities to understand the factors that influence race outcomes, optimize team strategies, and predict competitive dynamics. The intersection of data science and motorsport has transformed how teams approach race preparation, real-time decision-making, and long-term development, making Formula 1 one of the most data-intensive sports in the world. Each Grand Prix weekend produces millions of data points from practice sessions, qualifying rounds, and race day, creating a rich analytical landscape for exploring performance patterns, technological advantages, and competitive strategies.

The significance of data analysis in Formula 1 extends beyond team operations to influence regulatory decisions, broadcast coverage, and fan engagement. Teams invest heavily in data infrastructure, employing hundreds of engineers and analysts who process telemetry data in real-time during races and conduct detailed post-race analysis to gain competitive advantages. Race strategy decisions regarding tire compounds, pit stop timing, fuel loads, and overtaking opportunities increasingly rely on sophisticated predictive models that synthesize historical performance data, real-time conditions, and competitor behavior. The sport's technical regulations evolve annually, creating new challenges and opportunities for teams to exploit data-driven insights in car development and race execution. Furthermore, the global nature of Formula 1, with races held across diverse circuits ranging from high-speed tracks like Monza to technical street circuits like Monaco, produces varied performance characteristics that demand adaptive analytical approaches. Understanding these patterns through comprehensive data analysis reveals how track characteristics, weather conditions, tire management, and technical innovations interact to determine competitive outcomes.

Current research in Formula 1 data science has explored various dimensions of race performance, including lap time prediction, optimal pit stop strategies, tire degradation modeling, and driver performance evaluation. Academic studies have applied machine learning techniques to predict race winners, classify driver skill levels, and optimize team strategies based on historical data patterns. Industry practitioners have developed sophisticated simulation tools that model race scenarios, enabling teams to test strategic options before making critical decisions during live races. The availability of public timing data through official channels and community-developed APIs like FastF1 has democratized access to Formula 1 telemetry, enabling researchers and enthusiasts to conduct independent analyses and develop innovative analytical methodologies. Despite these advances, significant opportunities remain to deepen understanding of performance factors, particularly in areas such as tire compound selection under varying conditions, the impact of aerodynamic regulations on competitive balance, and the quantification of driver contributions relative to car performance.

This data science project investigates Formula 1 racing performance through comprehensive analysis of the 2024 season, focusing on lap times, tire strategies, driver performance, and race dynamics across multiple Grand Prix events. The analysis examines how various factors including tire compound selection, tire degradation over race distance, track characteristics, and race phase influence lap time performance and competitive outcomes. By gathering and analyzing official timing data, this research seeks to uncover patterns in racing performance that enhance understanding of competitive dynamics. The project utilizes data gathered through the FastF1 API, which provides access to official Formula 1 timing data including lap times, sector times, speed measurements, tire information, and race positions, creating a robust foundation for multi-dimensional analysis.

Data Preparation & Exploratory Data Analysis

Data Collection

FastF1 API

Data for this project was gathered using the FastF1 Python library, which provides programmatic access to official Formula 1 timing data. FastF1 retrieves data from the F1 live timing service, offering comprehensive telemetry information including lap times, sector times, speed measurements, tire data, and positional information.

API Website: https://github.com/theOehrly/Fast-F1

Races Analyzed: Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, Australia, Japan, Monaco (2024 Season)

Data Types: Lap times, sector times, speed measurements, tire compounds, tire age, race positions

Raw Data Sample

Sample of raw data retrieved from FastF1 API showing lap times, tire information, and performance metrics before cleaning.

Data Cleaning Process

The data cleaning process involved several critical steps to ensure data quality:

Missing Value Treatment: Removed rows with missing critical lap time data

Outlier Removal: Filtered out anomalous laps (>200 seconds) likely representing pit stops or incidents

Sector Time Imputation: Filled missing sector times using median values grouped by driver and track

Speed Data Handling: Imputed missing speed trap measurements using driver-track medians

Feature Engineering: Created derived features including lap phase, average speed, and tire freshness indicators
